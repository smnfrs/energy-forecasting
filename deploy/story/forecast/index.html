Tomorrow's forecast, explained

German power prices have grown far more volatile — and good forecasts are how the grid keeps up.

German energy generation needs to adapt for the 21st century, but renewables create challenges. The increasing proportion of renewables in the energy mix, together with more volatile commodity prices, has caused the volatility of electricity prices to explode. To cope with this volatility, we need a flexible energy system, and that flexibility requires high-quality forecasts. This page walks through the last 12 months of data, then explains what the model is forecasting for tomorrow and why.
